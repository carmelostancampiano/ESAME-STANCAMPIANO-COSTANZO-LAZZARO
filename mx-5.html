“It Just Feels Right.” (1990–1995)

Mazda MX-5 Miata I

The first generation of the Mazda Miata was inspired by classic roadsters such as the MGB, the Fiat 124 Spider, and the Lotus Elan. Indeed, Mazda engineers disassembled an Elan while designing the original Miata. The NA was introduced powered by a 1.6-liter twin-cam four producing 116 hp.

From 1994 onwards, displacement was increased to 1.8 liters in its inline four, now producing 128 hp. Visually, the earlier versions, known as the NA6, are scarcely distinguishable from the later NA8 save minor badge differences - the Miata script to the right of the rear license plate is painted red on the NA8, black on the NA6. In Japan, it was sold as the Eunos Roadster.

The NA generation of Miatas was succeeded by the MX-5 Miata NB for the model year 1999.

  • Engine :1.8 liter (inline 4)
  • Horsepower :129 hp
  • Drivetrain :RWD
  • Top Speed :197 Km/h - 122 Mph
  • 0 to 100 km/h (0 to 62 mph) :8.7 s
